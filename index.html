Learning to Surf for Beginners

This page covers the essentials to start surfing: safety, gear, and a simple step-by-step plan so your first sessions feel less confusing.

Get Started Top Tip
☀️

Warm Weather Sport

Surfing is easiest to learn in calm, warm conditions. Start at a beginner-friendly beach with smaller waves and lots of space.

🏄

Gear You Actually Need

A long soft-top board is easier and safer for learning. Use a leash, wax (or a traction pad), and a wetsuit if the water is cold.

✅

A Simple Progression

Learn in stages: practice paddling, then catching whitewater, then the pop-up. Standing up comes easier after you can control the board and your balance.

The #1 Beginner Tip

Most beginners try to stand too early and look down at their feet. Look forward — your body goes where your eyes go.

Quick tips

  • Look forward (not at the board)
  • Hands under your ribs when you pop up
  • Keep your weight centered over the board
  • Start on small whitewater waves

Helpful resource: NOAA weather and ocean info

Beginner Practice Plan

Two sessions that focus on control first, then standing up.

Session 1

Start close to shore in broken waves (whitewater). Practice paddling straight and riding the foam without standing. Then try a slow pop-up.

Safety reminders

Session 2

Practice pop-ups on the sand first, then in the water. Try angling the board slightly left or right and keep your knees bent.

Review the top tip

Ready to try it?

If you can, book one beginner lesson. If not, start with whitewater, practice pop-ups on land, and keep sessions short so you don’t burn out.
